About Me

I'm a cyber-maverick blurring boundaries and bending the rules of disciplines. Enrolled since Fall 2023 as a PhD student at Concordia University in Tio'tia:keh/Montréal, I'm a member of the Centre for Sensory Studies, the Hexagram network, the Milieux Institute, and the Applied AI Institute. I'm a proud SSHRC Doctoral Fellow even though I skipped classes a lot when I was in high school.

I practice ethnographic research-creation in the realm Neuro-AI. I'm crafting interactive installations and conducting performance research with GenAI and neurotechnologies. The idea is to create queer cyber-ecosystems as transdisciplinary spaces of improvisation with embodied, analog and computational instruments.

I study scientists and collaborate with artists to enact new-old, more-than-human ways of being. I'm also a musician and a coder at times. My contemplative practice is the glue to my various practices and identities, and it is a key element to my creative process. This website contains a non-exhaustive selection of my projects, publications, and insights. It is an ephemeral archive.

Current focus

Mechanical Meanderings

Mechanical Meanderings is an interactive installation that explores embodiement of metaphors and self-perception. Participants speak words which are analyzed by AI systems that formats their query into text-to-image prompts. These prompts are used in real-time with the camera capturing movements, blending their silhouettes with their metaphors. The installation is a solo project of mine, currently in development. It was exhbited at MUTEK Montréal 2024 and at MTL Connect 2024.

Meteomythosophy

Meteomythosophy is a transdisciplinary framework for immersive performances, connecting neuro-computational arts, religious studies, and laboratory theater. It uses meteorological metaphors to express the fluid and unpredictable aspects of consciousness and sense-making. The project was showcased at Embodied Interventions Festival 2024. It is a collaboration between Paolo Gruni, Thomas Seibel and I.
